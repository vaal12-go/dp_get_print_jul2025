India is the most populous country in the world, with close to 1.451 billion people as of 1 July 2024. India is closely followed by China, having a population of over 1.419 billion. Only these two nations have crossed the one billion mark, rest of countries are below 500 mn mark. The other five countries having a total number of humans above 200 mn are the United States of America, Indonesia, Pakistan, Nigeria, and Brazil.

Holy See (Vatican City) is the smallest country in the world. The population of Vatican City is approximately 496, and it is the only nation with a population below the one thousand marks.

As of 2024, 16 countries have a population above 100 million. Out of them, eight are located in Asia; four are in Africa, two in North America, and one each in Europe and South America. Viet Nam is the latest country to cross 100 mn population. Thirty-one countries have a population of more than 50 mn, while 95 have at least ten mn. The top five countries constitute about 46% of the global population; the top ten make up close to 57%, Top 20 countries add up to over 69%. One hundred five smallest countries/territories only contribute one percent of the global population, and 176 lowest ranked constitute only 10% of the total world population.

India would be the top contributor to the total world population increment in 2024. India accounts for 18.57 percent of total global increment. Indian population increased by 13 million, followed by Nigeria at 4.82 million. With 3.87 mn, 3.52 mn, and 3.41 mn, Pakistan, Democratic Republic of the Congo and Ethiopia are the 3rd, 4th, and 5th largest contributors to net population growth.

Out of 237 countries/territories projected by the UN, the population of 62 countries and territories would decrease in 2024. The combined decreased population of these 56 regions is 6,544,849. These 62 countries/territories account for 27.6% of the global population. China would record the highest decline in the population of 3.18 mn, followed by Russian Federation, Japan, Poland, and Germany with decrement over 200k.
